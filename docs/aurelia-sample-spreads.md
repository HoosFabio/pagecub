# Chapter 1: Not Sleepy Yet

**Summary:** Aurelia is bursting with energy at bedtime and tells Mama she cannot possibly sleep because she heard a dragon outside her window.

## Page 1
Aurelia was NOT sleepy. Not even a little bit.

She stood in her favorite yellow dress and her rainbow rainboots. Her brown hair tumbled past her shoulders, curling at the tips, half of it flopping right over one eye the way it always did.

She bounced on her toes. She wiggled her fingers. She made a silly face at her stuffed bunny.

"Bedtime, sweet girl!" Mama called from the doorway.

Aurelia's big blue-grey eyes went wide. Her mouth stretched into the biggest grin you ever saw, the kind that showed her one special dimple on her right cheek.

"But Mama," she said, "I can't go to bed yet!"

## Page 2
Mama put her hands on her hips and tried not to smile. "And why not?"

Aurelia leaned in close, like she had the most important secret in the whole world. She cupped her hands around her mouth and whispered very loudly.

"I think I heard a DRAGON outside!"

She pointed one finger straight at her window. Her eyes sparkled. Her dimple deepened.

Mama shook her head and laughed. "A dragon, huh?"

"A real one, Mama! With wings and everything!" Aurelia bounced again, her rainbow rainboots thumping on the floor. "I have to go check. I just HAVE to."

She was already tiptoeing toward the window, fingers reaching for the curtain. Something outside glowed soft and silver, just behind the glass.

# Chapter 3: A Tiny Dragon Friend

**Summary:** Aurelia climbs through her window onto the golden path of the Moonlight Kingdom and meets a tiny friendly dragon who lands on her shoulder and becomes her companion.

## Page 1
Aurelia giggled and climbed right through the window onto the sparkly golden path.

Her rainbow rainboots went crunch, crunch on the glittering ground. The air smelled like warm cookies and fresh rain all swirled together.

Tiny star-flowers tickled her ankles as she walked. Everything glowed soft and silver under the big round moon.

Then she heard a little sound above her. Fwip, fwip, fwip. Something small was flying down from the sky, coming closer and closer.

Aurelia looked up and held very still.

## Page 2
A tiny dragon landed right on her shoulder!

He was no bigger than a kitten, with shimmery green and gold scales and big round eyes that blinked at her. His little wings folded up neatly against his back.

"Hello!" said Aurelia.

The dragon tilted his head. Then he purred, soft and rumbly, like a happy cat.

Aurelia reached up and gently scratched under his chin. He closed his eyes and leaned into her fingers. His scales felt warm and smooth, like river stones in the sun.

"I think we're going to be friends," Aurelia whispered.

The little dragon chirped. Together they looked down the golden path winding deeper into the kingdom.

# Chapter 5: The Sleeping Star

**Summary:** Hazel points out the Sleeping Star, which has gone dim and left the Moonlight Kingdom in darkness. Without its gentle glow, all the little creatures are too scared to sleep, and someone must climb the tall hill to wake it.

## Page 1
Aurelia's eyes went wide. "Help you?" she asked.

Hazel nodded and pointed up at the sky. High above the kingdom, one little star hung among all the others. But this star was different. It was pale and dim, and its tiny eyes were closed tight.

"That is the Sleeping Star," said Hazel. "It used to glow so bright and warm that every creature in the kingdom felt safe at night. But now it has fallen asleep, and it won't wake up."

Aurelia looked down at the kingdom below. Little houses sat in shadows. Tiny faces peeked from dark windows, looking worried and small.

The little dragon on her shoulder gave a soft, sad chirp.

## Page 2
"Without the star's glow, the whole kingdom stays dark," Hazel said quietly. "All the little creatures are too scared to close their eyes. They need that gentle light to feel safe."

Aurelia knew that feeling. Sometimes her own room felt too dark at night.

She looked at the tiny faces in the windows. A fluffy owl with round worried eyes. A bunny hugging its own ears. A little mushroom fellow hiding behind a flower pot.

Her heart squeezed.

"Someone needs to climb the tall hill and wake that star," said Hazel, looking right at Aurelia.

Aurelia swallowed. The hill was very, very tall. But those little creatures needed help.

"Maybe," she whispered, "maybe I could try."
